THE FIRST ORDER

PRESIDENT'S OFFICE

Taxation Policy Document

Policy Title: Comprehensive Taxation Policy for the First Order Fund - Collective Prosperity Fund

Effective Date: 23/05/2024

Policy Overview

Pursuant to the directives of the President's Office of the First Order, this document establishes the Comprehensive Taxation Policy for the Collective Prosperity Fund (CPF). This policy outlines the taxation framework for all profits and incomes derived from interest, dividends, rent, and other sources related to the First Order. The policy is designed to promote fairness and efficiency in tax collection, with specific provisions for refunding taxes in cases of registered losses.

Section 1: Scope of Taxable Transactions

1.1 Taxable Income: All profits or income earned by any entity or individual from interest, dividends, rent, or any other source connected to the First Order shall be subject to taxation under this policy.

1.2 Exemptions: Voluntary and mandatory deposits to the CPF shall be exempt from taxation. A standard deduction of ₹250 applies to all incomes or profits in a financial year unless explicitly waived off.

Section 2: Threshold for Taxation

2.1 Minimum Taxable Amount: Taxation is applicable only to income or profits that exceed the standard deduction of ₹250 in a financial year.

2.2 Exclusion of Minor Amounts: Amounts below the standard deduction are not subject to taxation, simplifying the tax process and focusing on substantial income sources.

Section 3: Tax Rate

3.1 Flat Tax Rate: A uniform tax rate of one-third (33%) shall be applied to all taxable income and profits exceeding the standard deduction of ₹250.

3.2 Discretionary Taxation: The government reserves the right to impose additional taxes on any entity or individual at any time during the financial year if deemed necessary for the fund’s stability or public interest.

Section 4: Tax Collection and Refund Mechanism

4.1 Tax Collection Schedule: Taxes on eligible income must be calculated and remitted no later than January 13th of each year, based on the total receipts of the preceding financial year. On select financial instruments, government may direct the asset manager to deduct tax at source.

4.2 Refund of Taxes in Case of Losses: If the financial books of an individual or entity registered under the CPF show a loss, the government shall refund an amount equivalent to the taxes paid on the quantum of loss incurred. Refunds will be processed upon submission of audited financial statements and other required documentation.

Section 5: Compliance and Reporting Requirements

5.1 Record-Keeping: All taxpayers must maintain accurate and detailed records of taxable transactions, including interest, dividends, rent, and other related income.

5.2 Annual Reporting: Taxpayers are required to submit an annual report to the tax authority by January 13th, detailing all taxable income, the amount of tax due, and proof of payment.

5.3 Audits and Penalties: The tax authority shall conduct regular audits to ensure compliance with this policy. Penalties for late payment, underpayment, or non-compliance shall be enforced based on established models within the First Order taxation system, including fines and interest on overdue taxes.

Section 6: General Provisions

6.1 Policy Amendments: This policy may be amended at the discretion of the President’s Office, with any changes communicated to taxpayers promptly.

6.2 Legal Jurisdiction: All disputes arising under this policy shall be subject to the jurisdiction of the designated legal authorities of the First Order.

Conclusion

This taxation policy for the Collective Prosperity Fund is designed to ensure an equitable and efficient tax system that supports the financial health of the First Order. By integrating key elements of transparency, compliance, and fairness, this policy aims to bolster collective prosperity.
